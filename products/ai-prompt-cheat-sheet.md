## WHY MOST PROMPTS FAIL

Bad prompt: "Write me a marketing email."
Result: Generic, forgettable, unusable.

Good prompt: Uses one of the 8 patterns below.
Result: Copy that sounds like YOU, converts, and saves hours.

The difference is structure. Every great prompt has a **Role**, a **Task**, **Context**, and a **Constraint**. The 8 patterns below are the fastest ways to build that structure — memorize them and you'll out-prompt 99% of ChatGPT users.

## THE 8 POWER PATTERNS

---

### 1. ROLE PLAY (Instant Expert Mode)
**Pattern:** "You are a [specific expert]. [Task]."

**Why it works:** Forces the model into a specialized knowledge frame, cutting out generic filler.

**Example:**
> "You are a direct-response copywriter with 15 years of experience writing email campaigns for SaaS products. Write a 5-email welcome sequence for a $47/month project management tool targeting freelancers."

**Upgrade it:** Add stakes — "Your client is paying you $5,000 for this sequence. Do not disappoint."

---

### 2. CHAIN OF THOUGHT (Step-by-Step Reasoning)
**Pattern:** "Think step by step before answering: [complex task]."

**Why it works:** Forces the model to reason before concluding — eliminates shallow, wrong answers on logic-heavy problems.

**Example:**
> "Think step by step: I have $500 to spend on ads for a $97 digital product with a 3% conversion rate. What is my break-even ROAS, how many clicks do I need to buy, and what is the maximum I should bid per click on Google?"

**Best for:** Math, strategy, debugging, business decisions.

---

### 3. FEW-SHOT EXAMPLES (Match My Style)
**Pattern:** "Here are 3 examples of [X]. Now write a new one in the same style: [examples]."

**Why it works:** Shows > tells. The model matches tone, format, and style with near-perfect accuracy.

**Example:**
> "Here are 3 subject lines I've written that got 40%+ open rates:
> - 'You're doing email wrong (here's proof)'
> - 'I made $4,200 from one Google Doc'
> - 'The 10-minute trick that tripled my conversions'
> Write 10 more subject lines in this exact style for an email about AI productivity tools."

**Best for:** Copywriting, social posts, any brand-voice-sensitive task.

---

### 4. CONSTRAINT FORCING (Creativity Through Limits)
**Pattern:** "[Task] but you CANNOT use [constraint]."

**Why it works:** Constraints eliminate lazy defaults and force original thinking.

**Example:**
> "Write a landing page headline for a budgeting app, but you cannot use the words 'save money', 'financial freedom', 'stress-free', or 'take control'."

**Upgrade it:** Stack constraints — "Under 10 words. No questions. Must include a number."

---

### 5. PERSPECTIVE FLIP (Devil's Advocate Mode)
**Pattern:** "Argue against [your position/idea] as convincingly as possible."

**Why it works:** Surfaces objections before your customers raise them. Invaluable for refining offers, copy, and strategy.

**Example:**
> "I'm launching a $297 course teaching freelancers to use AI tools. Argue the strongest possible case for why NO freelancer should buy this course."

**Then follow up:** "Now write the counter-argument to every objection you just listed."

---

### 6. OUTPUT FORMATTING (Structured Delivery)
**Pattern:** "Output this as a [format] with [specific structure]."

**Why it works:** The model defaults to walls of text. Specifying format = immediately usable output.

**Power formats:**
- "Output as a markdown table with columns: [col1], [col2], [col3]"
- "Give me a JSON object with keys: title, description, price, cta"
- "Format as a numbered list. Each item: bold headline, then 2-sentence explanation."
- "Write this as a Twitter/X thread. Each tweet max 280 characters. Start with a hook."

---

### 7. ITERATIVE REFINEMENT (The 3-Pass Method)
**Pattern:** Write it → Critique it → Rewrite it. In ONE prompt.

**Why it works:** Embeds the editing loop inside the generation, giving you polished output in one shot.

**Template:**
> "Write a [deliverable]. Then critique it harshly — identify every weak word, cliche, and missed opportunity. Then rewrite it incorporating all your critiques. Label the three sections DRAFT, CRITIQUE, and FINAL."

**Best for:** Copy, emails, proposals — anything where quality matters.

---

### 8. CONTEXT INJECTION (Feed It Your World)
**Pattern:** Paste your real data, then ask a question about it.

**Why it works:** ChatGPT's generic knowledge is useless without your specifics. Your actual numbers, customer feedback, and brand voice make every output 10x more relevant.

**What to inject:**
- Your last 5 customer reviews (verbatim)
- Your product's feature list
- Your target customer's job title + biggest pain
- Your competitor's pricing page (copy-paste it)

**Example:**
> "Here are 5 real reviews of my product: [paste reviews]. Identify the 3 most common emotional pain points expressed. Then write a Facebook ad that speaks directly to the #1 pain point using the exact language customers used."

## COMBO MOVES (Stack Patterns for Maximum Output)

| Goal | Patterns to Stack |
|---|---|
| Sales email that converts | Role Play + Few-Shot + Iterative Refinement |
| Competitor research brief | Context Injection + Perspective Flip + Output Formatting |
| Landing page from scratch | Role Play + Constraint Forcing + Iterative Refinement |
| Viral social content | Few-Shot + Constraint Forcing + Output Formatting |
| Business strategy decision | Chain of Thought + Perspective Flip |

## THE MASTER TEMPLATE

Copy this every time you start a complex prompt:

```
You are a [ROLE with specific expertise].
Your task is to [SPECIFIC DELIVERABLE].
Context: [WHO this is for + WHAT problem it solves + any relevant data].
Constraints: [Word count / format / tone / things to avoid].
Output format: [Exact structure you want].
Think step by step before writing.
```

## QUICK REFERENCE: POWER WORDS THAT IMPROVE ANY PROMPT

**Add specificity:** "exactly", "specifically", "precisely", "in detail"
**Add stakes:** "this is for a paying client", "our conversion rate depends on this"
**Force originality:** "do not use cliches", "avoid generic advice", "be counterintuitive"
**Force brevity:** "in under X words", "no fluff", "cut every unnecessary sentence"
**Force action:** "make it actionable", "include exact steps", "give me something I can use today"
